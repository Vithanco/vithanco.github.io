How to Benefit Mapping for your Change Initiative

Estimated reading time: 2 minutes.

This is the continuation of this article](/Methods/typesOfBenefits) where we looked at different types of benefits. Today we will look at how to use a benefit within your change initiative. By the way, when I say "change initiative", then I refer to any of the following (and maybe more):

  • A project
  • A program
  • A Product development
  • An agile initiative

Benefits and Business Case

We all have used benefits in business cases for a new change initiative. The business case is the document that is used to justify the investment in a change initiative. And isn't it funny how benefits seem to be important for the business case, but then they are not mentioned again? Sometimes, somebody finds out towards the end that the benefits weren't as big as expected. But if the project delivered the agreed scope, then overall everyone is happy even when the benefits were missed.

Am I too cynical? I hope so. But I have seen it too often. And I have been guilty of it myself. The question is: Is this how benefits should be used? If they are important during the business case, are they unimportant ever after? I don't think so.

Benefits for the Project

So, what is the role of benefits during the project? I think there are three main roles:

  • Benefits are used to define the success of the project
  • Benefits are used to define the scope of the project

Benefits are used to define the success of the project

It should be self evident that after using benefits to approve the project the benefits The project aims at benefits and hence the realisation of the benefit should be a success if the benefits are delivered. But what does that mean? It means that the benefits are delivered to the extent that the benefit owner is happy. And that is the key: the benefit owner needs to be happy. If the benefit owner is not happy, then the project is not a success. And if the benefit owner is happy, then the project is a success. It is that simple.
